I am using this to sharpen my coding and analytics skills through research attempting to find truer player valuations. The abstract lays out the intent and limits of the work so far. 

Here are a few thoughts outside of the abstract: 
Open to any and all collaborations. Grassroots work gets stronger by actually spreading horizontally. Criticize away, this is only meant to provoke some thought. 

I've seen some neat refinements to the aging/arc. Right now just using linear regression, future iterations will attempt to account for survivorship.
Likewise I like the idea of moving this beyond outcome based metrics like variations of FIP especially as catcher framing and umpire impacts will be muted in 2026 onward. 
However, a lot of people underestimate the power of outcome because there is a lot of good that a club will want to see in there like Grit, determination, that aren't always seen in Stuff+, Pitching+ etc.

One of the most overlooked aspects of valuaiton is discounting. Almost all amateur baseball research tries to find value where others don't see it. 
That's only the numerator of the equation. I'd like future drafts to find better methods to iterate timing of roster construction.
I think the Padres use Player Development and comparative advantage in Trading more readily than most clubs. Valuations and trade are known, but how the discount to master timing is somehting I would like to try to replicate. 

Lastly, this will likely be a separate project but I would like to explore the demand side of pitching in free agency.
The nature of this research assumes that supply is normally distributed and compensation is based on availability and necessity. 

I believe with the playoffs incuding more teams and rounds the incentive for more teams to "buy" the deadline, has and will continue to increase demand for pitching
How teams cluster to what points of availability on the distribution curve however is fascinating. 
Cluster analysis and some game theory could help refine supply and demand charts, but figuring out how and why teams pay what they do and when they do is hard to model.
